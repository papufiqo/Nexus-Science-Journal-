Journal Information

A decade of scientific excellence

Nexus Science Journal was established in 2014 with a mission to democratise access to high-quality scientific research. As a fully open-access publication, every article we publish is immediately and permanently free to read, download, and share under Creative Commons licensing.

Our rigorous double-blind peer review process ensures that only the highest-quality, methodologically sound research reaches our readers. We are committed to research integrity, reproducibility, and editorial independence.

We particularly welcome submissions that bridge disciplinary boundaries — studies at the intersection of ecology and medicine, geochemistry and public health, or climate science and epidemiology are among our most-cited contributions.

Original Research Earth Science

Permafrost thaw dynamics and methane flux amplification in Siberian tundra: evidence from 15-year field monitoring

Ivanova, M.K. · Zhang, W. · Bergström, A. · Nkemdirim, D.C.

We present a multi-decadal analysis of permafrost degradation rates and associated greenhouse gas emissions from the West Siberian Plain. Using a network of 47 monitoring stations, we document a 340% increase in methane flux between 2009 and 2024, with non-linear acceleration observed post-2018 coinciding with record summer temperatures.

Efficacy of next-generation CAR-T cell therapy in treatment-refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma: a phase III multicentre trial

Osei-Bonsu, K. · Yamamoto, T. · García-López, P. · Hassan, F.A.

This randomised controlled trial enrolled 324 patients across 18 international centres. Modified CAR-T constructs incorporating novel costimulatory domains demonstrated an overall response rate of 78.4% compared to 31.2% for standard-of-care, with manageable toxicity profiles and a median progression-free survival of 22.3 months.

CRISPR-Cas12a applications in gene regulation: mechanisms, off-target effects, and therapeutic frontiers — a systematic review

Andriessen, E.L. · Kimura, S. · Adeyemi, O.T. · Petrov, V.

We systematically reviewed 312 primary studies on CRISPR-Cas12a from 2018–2024, synthesising data on editing efficiency, specificity profiles, delivery mechanisms, and clinical translation progress. Meta-analysis reveals that lipid nanoparticle delivery achieves 94.7% efficiency in hepatic targets with a significantly improved off-target rate compared to earlier AAV-based approaches.

Original Research Earth Science

Deep-time ocean acidification events and mass extinction correlates: new evidence from Triassic carbonate platforms

Patel, R.S. · Lefebvre, C. · Morozov, I.A. · Subramaniam, K.

Geochemical proxies from 14 Tethyan carbonate platform sections reveal a cascade of acidification pulses preceding the end-Triassic extinction. Carbon isotope excursions of up to −8.4‰ and boron isotope-derived pH reconstructions document ocean pH declining to 7.3 over periods as short as 50,000 years — with alarming parallels to present-day trajectories.

Original Research Medicine

Gut microbiome composition as a predictor of antidepressant treatment response in major depressive disorder: a longitudinal cohort study

Kowalski, A.M. · Singh, P. · Moreau, D. · Al-Rashidi, K.

In a cohort of 891 MDD patients followed over 24 weeks, baseline microbiome diversity indices (Shannon H > 3.8) predicted SSRI treatment response with 71.2% sensitivity and 68.9% specificity. Enrichment of Lactobacillus rhamnosus and Bifidobacterium longum subspecies emerged as the strongest individual predictors.

Original Research Natural Science

Novel photocatalytic mechanisms in TiO₂-graphene hybrid nanomaterials under visible-light irradiation for environmental remediation

Ferreira, J.P. · Kim, D.H. · Oluwaseun, A.B. · Schneider, R.T.

We synthesised a series of TiO₂-graphene hybrid composites and elucidated charge transfer dynamics using ultrafast transient absorption spectroscopy. The optimised composite (TG-15) achieved 98.7% degradation of pharmaceutical micropollutants within 60 minutes under solar-spectrum illumination, representing a 4.2-fold improvement over pure TiO₂.

Our Peer Review Model

Nexus Science Journal uses double-blind peer review as default, with optional open peer review (where reviewer identity is disclosed post-publication). We aim to return decisions within 21 days of submission.

  • Double-blind review: both author and reviewer identities concealed
  • Minimum two independent expert reviewers per manuscript
  • Statistical review for all quantitative studies
  • Portable review option: import reviews from other journals

Open Access

Science belongs to everyone

Every article published in Nexus Science Journal is immediately and permanently open access — no subscription, no paywall, no embargo. Our commitment to open science is unconditional.

🔓

Immediate Open Access for All Published Research

Under our diamond open access model, readers and institutions pay nothing to access articles, and authors in low- and middle-income countries can apply for full APC waivers. We believe removing financial barriers is essential to achieving equitable global science.

APC Waiver Application →
  • Instantly discoverable via Google Scholar, Scopus, PubMed and all major indices
  • Permanent archiving via PubMed Central, Zenodo, and CLOCKSS
  • Persistent DOI registration with CrossRef
  • Machine-readable full text via our OAI-PMH endpoint
  • Re-use permitted under Creative Commons CC BY 4.0
  • Published version is always the final, peer-reviewed record
  • Preprint compatibility: link your arXiv/bioRxiv preprint to the final article
CC BY 4.0
Creative Commons Attribution

The default licence for all articles. Anyone may copy, distribute, adapt, and build upon the work for any purpose, provided appropriate attribution is given. Full commercial reuse is permitted.

CC BY-NC 4.0
Attribution Non-Commercial

Available upon author request for specific content types. Allows distribution and adaptation but restricts commercial use of the published work.

CC0 / Public Domain
No Rights Reserved

For datasets and supplementary materials where authors wish to maximise reuse potential. No restrictions on how the material is used, remixed, or redistributed.
